---
title: "面向高通量新材料发现的自动化实验"
---

随着全球能源需求的不断攀升，推动交通电气化以及实现可再生能源的高效利用已成为当务之急。这一趋势对低成本、高能量密度的储能技术提出了迫切需求。传统的锂离子电池材料研究主要局限于结构高度有序的材料体系，往往忽略了通过调控结构的“有序-无序”度来进一步优化性能的潜力。本课题组致力于结合先进的表征技术与材料结构-性能预测模型，通过多尺度的结构调控策略，开发高性能的正极材料与固态电解质。
To address the escalating global demand for energy and facilitate the transition toward transportation electrification and renewable energy integration, there is an urgent need for low-cost, energy-dense energy storage solutions. Historically, research on Li-ion battery materials has predominantly focused on highly ordered structures, often overlooking the opportunities enabled by tuning structural disorder. By integrating advanced characterization techniques with structure–property modeling, our group aims to develop high-performance cathodes and solid-state electrolytes through multi-scale structural design.
